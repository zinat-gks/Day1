01

Do older drivers gain more places than younger ones?

Barely — age hardly matters.

  • "Gaining places" = finishing higher than you started (e.g. start 15th, finish 8th = +7)
  • Old vs young drivers climb almost the same — the link is just −0.02
  • Rookies climb a little more, but only because they tend to start near the back

It's the grid slot doing the work, not the driver's age.

02

Does starting on pole always mean a win?

No — pole wins just 43% of the time.

  • Track matters: Abu Dhabi/Singapore ~70%
  • Slipstream tracks like Monza ~30%
  • Pole is a big head start, never a guarantee

Where you race matters more than where you qualify.

03

Do Asian races cause more breakdowns than European ones?

No — that's an illusion.

  • Raw numbers: Europe 43% DNF vs Asia 21%

Compare like-for-like and the gap vanishes.

04

Do drivers from dominant F1 nations perform better?

Yes — about 40% more wins.

  • Win rate 4.7% vs 3.3%
  • Podium rate 13.7% vs 11.1%
  • Finish ~1 place higher on average

Mostly access to the best teams — not the passport.

05

Max Verstappen vs Lewis Hamilton — who's ahead?

Lewis on totals, Max on rate.

  • Both raced in 239 GPs: Hamilton finished ahead 134–105
  • Wins 106 vs 71 · poles 104 vs 48
  • Win rate: Max 29.6% just edges Lewis 27.4%

Lewis has the career; Max has the recent dominance.

06

So… is Lewis Hamilton the GOAT?

Yes — by the record books.

  • 106 wins · most ever
  • 206 podiums · most ever
  • 104 poles · 5,058 points

…though Juan Fangio's 41% win rate is still untouched.
