PROVISIONS OF THE YESSENOV LAUNCH PAD COMPETITIVE PROGRAM
1. GENERAL TERMS

1.1. The YESSENOV LAUNCH PAD Program (hereinafter referred to as the ‘Program’ provides up to ten
(10) grants for additional training aimed at developing hard skills and/or soft skills, limited to no more
than two (2) online/offline training courses, with a duration of up to twelve (12) months.

12. The Program is funded at the initiative of the Private foundation ‘The Shakhmardan Yessenov
Science and Education Foundation’ (hereinafter referred to as the ‘Foundation).

1.3. The Program is aimed at developing hard and soft skills that enable individuals to address
professional and life challenges at a higher level of quality.

1.4. In these Provisions, the term ‘Grant’ refers to financial support of up to six hundred thousand
(600,000) tenge awarded to the Program winners, who demonstrate the highest level of interest and
determination in pursuing additional education.

1.5. The winners of the Program are selected through an open Competition.

|. PROGRAM IMPLEMENTATION

2.1. The Program is long-term and is implemented in several stages:

2.1.1. The first stage is the competitive selection of the Program winners;

2.1.2. The second stage - the winners of the Competition independently search for online/offline training
courses to develop hard and/or soft skills;

2.1.3. The third stage is the training of the Program winners.
2.2. For conducting a competitive selection, the Foundation establishes an Expert commission, which
selects participants. The Expert commission selects scholarship recipients (winners) on the basis of the
results of two (2) rounds of the Competition.
2.3. The decision of the Expert commission is approved by the Foundation’s order which contains
officially certified list of the Program winners. The list of the Program winners is to be published on the
Foundation’s website: yessenovfoundation.org.
2.4. Applications for participation in the Program are accepted in the Kazakh, Russian and English
languages. The working language of the Program is Russian.

2.5. The Foundation has the right to refuse to provide funding for the Program training without
explaining the reasons for the refusal.

2.6. The Foundation awards up to ten (10) grants under the Program. The final decision on the
number of the Program grants is determined by the Foundation's Expert Commission.

2.7. The Foundation reserves the right to award fewer or more than ten (10) declared grants, should the
Foundation’s Expert Council make such a decision.

Ш. REQUIREMENTS FOR CANDIDATES

3.1. Only citizens of the Republic of Kazakhstan who are over eighteen (18) years of age, penultimate and
final year undergraduates, master’s students, doctoral students and working professionals in the
following areas* can participate in the Program:

- natural sciences, mathematics and statistics;

- information and communication technologies;

- engineering, manufacturing and construction industries;

- healthcare.

* according to the Classifier of Training Programs for Personnel with Higher and Post-Graduate
Education (Approved by the Order of the Minister of Education and Science of the Republic of
Kazakhstan as of October 13, 2018, No. 569).

The following qualities of applicants will be taken into consideration in determining the Program winners:
3.1.1. high intellectual potential;

3.1.2. willingness to develop hard skills;

5.1.5. a broad range of hard skills

3.1.4 focusing on efficiency;

3.1.5. desire and ability to take responsibility for the quality of his/her own life.

3.2. The candidate must declare and confirm the desire to improve his/her hard skills in the areas
specified in clause 3.1. of these Provisions, as well as justify the need to develop soft skills:


3.2.1. project management;

3.2.2. professional communication and negotiation;
5.2.5. presentation and public speaking mastery;
3.2.4. stress management;

3.2.5. time management;

3.2.6. emotional intelligence;

3.2.7. leadership and project team management;
5.2.8. others, in coordination with the Foundation.

3.3. To participate in the Competition, a candidate must:

5.5.1. Complete the online application form on the Foundation’s website: yessenovfoundation.org.

5.5.2. Attach the following documents to the application:

Required documents:

1) scan of the identity card;

2) an essay explaining why he/she needs additional skills and how they will affect his/her professional
and personal life;

3) resume/CV.

Additional documents:

1) a portfolio showcasing the candidate’s achievements and/or expressed interests in a specific area, in
accordance with clause 3.1. of these Provisions, and provide relevant evidence — no more than three (3)
documents.

2) recommendations — no more than one (1) document;

3) certificates confirming academic achievements, success in research and/or engineering activities,
participation in conferences, seminars of different levels - no more than three (3) documents*;

4) individual or co-authored publications - no more than two (2) publications;

5) diploma (last place of study), if available - no more than one (1) document;

6) social activities and volunteering certificates — no more than two (2) certificates*

*The Foundation considers a limited number of certificates. The candidate must independently
select the certificates that best showcase his/her achievements.

3.4. Applications are accepted on the Foundation’s website: yessenovfoundation.org from September
10 through October 14, 2025.

The deadline for acceptance of applications is October 14, 2025, 3:00 P.M. Astana time (GMT+5).
Applications received after the deadline will not be considered.

3.5. An application and essay can be submitted in the Kazakh, Russian or English languages.
Documents (certificates, diplomas, recommendations, etc.) can be provided in their original
language.

3.6. Submission of documents to participate in the Competition implies that the applicant shares the
goals of the Program, confirms his/her agreement with all the terms and conditions of the Program as
set forth in these Provisions, and is prepared to fully comply with them without any exceptions.

IV. STAGES OF COMPETITIVE SELECTION

4.1. The Competition consists of two (2) rounds. The best candidates are selected after each round.

4.2. The First round of the Competition is an application assessment.

All the applications received on time are reviewed and assessed by the Foundation’s employees and
independent experts from October 15 through October 27, 2025, in accordance with the following
criteria:

4.2.1. portfolio quality;

4.2.2. essay quality;

4.2.3. work experience;

4.2.4. participation in olympiads, conferences, exhibitions, seminars;

4.2.5. availability of publications;

4.2.6. foreign language proficiency;

4.2.7. social activities.



The results of the first round of the Competition will be communicated to the candidate via email. Тһе
list of candidates who have passed to the second round of the Competition is to be published on the
Foundation’s website: yessenovfoundation.org - no later than October 28, 2025.

4.3. Based on the results of the first round of the Competition, no more than twenty (20)
candidates are selected.

4.4. The second round of the Competition is an online individual and/or group interview.
The online interview is conducted by the Foundation’s employees and a psychologist from October 29
through November 7, 2025.

4.5. The following candidate qualities are assessed during this interview:

4.5.1. hard skills;

4.5.2. learning ability and the ability to quickly perceive and analyze new information;
4.5.3. determination;

4.5.4. the ability to adapt to a new situation and find non-standard solutions;

4.5.5. communication skills;

4.5.6. clarity and reasonableness of goal setting;

4.5.7. emotional intelligence;

4.5.8. others

4.6. The Foundation reserves the right not to comment on its decisions to the participants of the
Competition.

4.7. The Foundation makes a final decision on November 10, 2025. The list of the Program winners is to
be published on the Foundation’s website: yessenovfoundation.org.

4.8. Based on the results of the second round of the Competition, ten (10) winners of the Program
are selected.

V. SPONSORSHIP TERMS AND CONDITIONS

5.1. The Foundation assists the Program winners in finding high-quality further education courses to
enhance their hard and/or soft skills.

5.2. The Program winner must coordinate the selected courses with the Foundation. The Foundation
may engage an expert to evaluate the quality of the courses.

5.5. To undertake the selected courses, the Competition winner and the Foundation shall enter into a
Sponsorship Agreement (hereinafter referred to as the ‘Agreement’), which serves as the basis for the
Foundation’s disbursement of funds (Sponsorship assistance).

5.4. The Competition winner awarded an education grant must comply with the terms of the Agreement
concluded with the Foundation, including but not limited to:

1) attend courses according to the schedule and not miss classes without a valid reason;

2) responsibly complete all coursework and independent assignments as part of the training course;

3) take exams on time;

4) comply with all other obligations and rules of the educational institution where the Program winner is
enrolled in a training course;

5) disseminate information about the Foundation in any appropriate form that supports a positive
reputation of the Foundation, including through his/her own social media accounts, as well as distribute
information and advertising materials provided by the Foundation;

6) inform the Foundation about their personal achievements in hard and soft skills;

7) participate in blogs and other proposed activities as and when requested by the Foundation.

8) provide any information, including that related to participation in the Competition and the
implementation of the awarded grant (internship, scholarship, training, etc.), as and when requested by
the Foundation, no later than three (3) business days from the date the request is received at the email
address specified in the application;

9) provide assistance in the Foundation’s activities and programs as and when requested by the
Foundation;

10) provide personal photos, text, and video materials (from places of study/work/internships/social
events, etc.) as and when requested by the Foundation (people’s appearance and clothing in photos
