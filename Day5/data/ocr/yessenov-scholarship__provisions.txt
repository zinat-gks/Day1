PROVISIONS
OF THE SHAKHMARDAN YESSENOV FOUNDATION
“Academician Shakhmardan Yessenov Scholarship Competition 2026”

|. General Terms.
1.1. The Academician Shakhmardan Yessenov Scholarship Competition (hereinafter referred to as the
“Competition”) is held and financed at the initiative of the private foundation “Shakhmardan Yessenov
Science and Education Foundation” (hereinafter referred to as the “Foundation”), and is aimed to
stimulate the academic, scientific, and social activities of the citizens of the Republic of Kazakhstan
studying at Kazakhstani higher education institutions (HEls), in order to perpetuate the memory of
Shakhmardan Yessenov - an outstanding geoscientist, prominent statesman and public figure,
academician of the National Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Kazakhstan, doctor of geological
and mineralogical sciences, professor, Lenin Prize laureate, laureate of the State Prize of the Republic of
Kazakhstan, and Shokan Ualikhanov Prize laureate.
1.2. The Competition aims to promote the growth of professional and personal competencies, social
responsibility, and leadership skills among the best undergraduate students at higher education
institutions in the Republic of Kazakhstan.
1.3. In the Provisions, the term “Scholarship” refers to a monetary payment from the Foundation in the
amount of seventy thousand (70,000) tenge per month or eight hundred forty thousand (840,000)
tenge per year. The Scholarship is awarded by the Foundation to no more than twenty (20) Scholarship
holders (winners) of the Competition for a period of twelve (12) months, from May 2026 through April
2027.
1.4. The Scholarship holders (winners) are selected by the Foundation through an open Competition. The
Competition is held by the Foundation in the second semester of the current academic year. The
Foundation awards named Scholarships based on the Competition results.
1.5. The Program provides for a mandatory quota of two (2) Scholarships for students of Satbayev
University (KazNRTU named after К. I. Satpayev) where Academician Shakhmardan Yessenov studied
and taught.

|. Requirements for applicants for the Academician Shakhmardan Yessenov Named Scholarship.
2.1. The Competition is open to citizens of the Republic of Kazakhstan over the age of eighteen (18) who
are full-time students at Kazakhstani higher education institutions, whether on a state-funded or self-
funded basis.
2.2. Applicants for the Scholarship Competition may be:
a. undergraduate students, except those in the first and final years of study;
b. bachelor’s degree students at medical higher education institutions, except those in their first year of
study, if an internship is mandatory;
studying at Kazakhstani higher education institutions, according to the classifier of areas of
training for personnel with higher and postgraduate education* as provided by the legislation of
the Republic of Kazakhstan, in the following areas:
- 6805 natural sciences, mathematics and statistics;
- 6BOG information and communication technologies;
- 6807 engineering, manufacturing and construction industries;
- 6B10 healthcare.
Twenty (20) Scholarship winners will be selected from among the applicants.
2.3 Applicants studying in a bachelor’s degree program must have academic performance of “good” or
“excellent” (the average GPA must be at least 3.0). The GPA data of the applicants must be confirmed by
a transcript.
2.4. The Scholarship applicant must declare and provide documentary evidence of active participation in
the scientific and social life at their HEI.
2.5. Any Scholarship applicant who is a relative of a member of the HEI’s administration (such as the
Rector, Vice-Rector, member of the Academic Council, Institute Director, Dean, or Head of Department)



where the applicant studies must disclose this familial relationship in their application for the
Competition.

2.6. The awarding of the Scholarship does not affect the applicant’s right to receive a state scholarship,
including an increased scholarship.

2.7. The Competition is held annually. An applicant who has previously received the Scholarship also has
the right to participate in the Competition in subsequent years on general terms.

5.2. An Expert Commission is established to conduct the Competition and selects the Scholarship
holders (winners) based on the results of two (2) rounds of the Competition.

officially certified list of the Competition winners. The results of the rounds and the list of winners are
published on the Foundation’s website: yessenovfoundation.org.
3.4. The Scholarship is awarded by the Foundation once (1) a year.

№. Stages of competitive selection.

4.1. The Competition comprises two (2) rounds. The best applicants are revealed at the end of each
round.

4.2. The first round of the Competition is held from March 3 through March 18, 2026.

4.2.1. All applications received are reviewed and assessed in absentia by independent experts according
to the following criteria:

a. high academic potential (including erudition in the specified area of interest; novelty of approaches;
good academic results);

b. availability of scientific publications related to the speciality;

c. activity in student life;

d. volunteer experience;

e. other.

4.2.3. Applicants who advanced to the second round of the Competition receive an email letter. The list
of those qualified for the second round of the Competition is also published on the Foundation’s website
yessenovfoundation.org. Upon completion of the first round of the Competition, no more than fifty (50)
students will be selected. The results of the first qualifying round of the Competition will be announced
no later than March 20, 2026.

4.3. The second round of the Competition is held from March 26 through April 7, 2026.

4.3.1. The applicants selected for the second round of the Competition are invited to an online selection,
which is conducted as an individual interview and/or group case work. During this interview, the
applicants’ qualities are assessed through the observation of the following areas:

a. general communication;

b. system of values and attitudes, motivation;

c. general activity;

d. emotional intelligence;

e. understanding of the demands of the modern world;

f. overall psychological maturity;

g. thinking;

h. willingness of applicants to post motivating content on social media.

4.3.2. The duration of the individual interview is up to forty (40) minutes, and group work is up to one
hundred and twenty (120) minutes. If necessary, the Foundation may conduct additional testing of the
applicants. At the end of the second round of the Competition, no more than twenty (20) winners will be
selected.

4.3.3. The Foundation reserves the right not to comment on its decisions to the Competition
participants.

4.4. The list of the Scholarship holders (winners) of the Competition is published on the Foundation’s
website yessenovfoundation.org no later than April 8, 2026.

V. Terms of receiving and payment procedure of the Scholarship.
5.1. The Foundation enters into a sponsorship agreement with the Scholarship holder (winner) of the
Competition.


5.2. The recipient who has entered into a sponsorship agreement with the Foundation is obliged to
comply with the terms of the agreement, including, but not limited to:

1) to study at a higher education institution in accordance with its rules and requirements;

2) to keep up with studies at a higher education institution in all disciplines getting “good” and
“excellent” grades. As and when requested by the Foundation, academic performance data must be
confirmed by a transcript that the Recipient provides within five (5) calendar days from the date of the
request;

5) to actively participate in student scientific and practical conferences;

4) to be actively involved in social activities at the higher education institution;

5) to disseminate information about the Foundation in any appropriate form, including posts on their
social media accounts, while maintaining and promoting a positive image of the Foundation, and to
distribute informational and promotional materials provided by the Foundation;

6) to inform the Foundation of their achievements in professional activities and soft skills development;
7) to participate in blogs and other proposed activities upon the request of the Foundation;

8) to provide any information, including that related to participation in the competition and the
implementation of the awarded grant (internship, scholarship, training, etc.), upon the Foundation’s
request, no later than three (3) business days from the moment the request is received at the email
address specified in the application;

9) as and when requested by the Foundation, provide assistance in its activities and programs;

10) to provide personal photos, text, and video materials (from places of study, work, internships, social
events, etc.) upon request of the Foundation (ensuring that individuals in the photos and videos
appear neat and appropriately dressed for a secular environment), and to participate in photo and
video sessions during the Foundation’s events for the purpose of creating success stories for the
Foundation’s platforms and other publications aimed at inspiring the youth of Kazakhstan;

11) in performing their obligations under the Agreement, the Program winner and the Foundation’s
employees shall refrain from committing, and encouraging any actions that violate or contribute to the
violation of the legislation of the Republic of Kazakhstan, including in the field of combating corruption.
They shall not pay, offer to pay or authorize the payment of any money or valuables, directly or
indirectly, to any persons to influence their actions or decisions in order to obtain any unlawful
advantage or achieve other improper objectives.

5.2. The Scholarship is paid based on the agreement concluded between the Foundation and the
Scholarship holder (winner) of the Competition, by transferring funds to the Scholarship holder’s
(winner’s) bank account no later than the fifteenth (15th) day of the following month.

5.3. The Scholarship is paid monthly and only to active students at the higher education institutions in
the Republic of Kazakhstan. The Scholarship payment to the recipient (winner) of the Competition is
terminated by the Foundation if the student is on academic leave, etc., the sponsorship agreement
between the specified parties is considered terminated unilaterally on the initiative of the Foundation.
5.4. The Foundation reserves the right to verify the information provided by applicants and/or winners of
the Program by any legal means and to exclude any applicant from the Competition at any stage and/or
from the Program in the event that such information is found to be inaccurate.

5.5. The Foundation reserves the right not to provide explanations regarding the motives or reasons for
decisions made concerning grant applicants under the Program.
